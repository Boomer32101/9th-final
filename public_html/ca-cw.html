Captain America: Civil War

Sean Miller

This May Captain America: Civil War entered theaters and again like in Batman vs Superman : Dawn of Justice, the heroes become locked in a struggle against one another. I thought this movie did a better job making a movie about heroes fighting each other than Batman vs Superman . Though the ethics on both side have some flaws, you are pretty much following Captain America on this opposed to Batman vs Superman where you mostly follow Batman but Superman has more reason than Batman and the movie builds up to something that ends up becoming just a petty quarrel when Doomsday comes. While in Captain America: Civil War the battle of Captain America vs Iron Man is the main plot of the movie with no third power too great to change the plot. Both Batman vs Superman and Civil War were long movies but Civil War progressed more smoothly and the movie did not feel like it dragged on like in Batman vs Superman. In the past few years Disney has made a great film empire out of the Marvel movies and are doing a great job. I find the idea of all these movies being interconnected in the same storyline unique and cool. They did a good job introducing the Black Panther who I think has some great potential in future Marvel movies and Spider-man as well had a good introduction in this movie and I think this fun, young version of Peter Parker has great potential. Ant-Man like in his own movie brought humor into the movie while the main characters still got to shine and do their thing. The fact that the sides weren’t completely clear allowed some characters to have some inter conflicts and shifting of sides. Overall it was a pretty good movie and I think that you should go see it and bring your family too.

Owen Vandevelde

Captain America: Civil War hit theaters May 6th, and was great, but it still had some flaws. First of all, it didn’t follow the Civil War comic that much, probably because they didn’t have nearly as many characters as in the comic. However, This meant no inter-dimensional prison, no Captain America dying, no Thor clone, and no Spider-Man publicly revealing his identity. This doesn’t make the movie bad, but it is annoying to know that there is two different stories. Another difference is that Baron Zemo drives the conflict by framing Bucky Barnes (The Winter Soldier) unlike the idea of going public because of a disaster like in the comic. With all these differences, the movies was still good, it had a solid plot and the dialog was funny and entertaining. It was obviously better than it’s competition Batman V Superman and I would recommend that you see this movie while still in theaters.
