The Martian

Sean Miller

In the movie the Martian, astronaut Mark Watney gets stranded on Mars and has to survive on his own. It contains conflict on what the control center people back on Earth should do including the crew who Mark is a member of, survival on Mars using SCIENCE!!!! And also containing a nice bit of comedy on top of all that. Both Michael Pena(~ on n)(he was in Ant-Man and Fury) and Sebastian Stan(the winter soldier) were in it but their roles were small but the probably had other movies to be in it and so they don’t take too much away from Matt Damon but was nice to see them in this movie. I thought that the movie was kept interesting by creating conflict and things not always going the right way. It also taught me that astronauts can fly like iron man. I think the hard thing about movies like this is keeping it exciting and not a movie about how to survive on Mars if it really happened which I think this movie did a pretty good job on.

Owen Vandevelde

The Martian is the movie adaptation of Andy Weir’s novel, “The Martian”. This movie took the viewers to Mars to watch Mark Watney struggle to survive after his crew left him, thinking he was dead. He awakes on the surface of Mars, alone. Watney has to do surgery on himself, and figure out how to make food that will last him until he is rescued. The story is very scientifically accurate and the CGI looks very real. The movie takes a look at how isolation and desperation will drive someone to overcome a looming task. One of the coolest parts of the movie is when Watney has to grow food so he can survive longer on Mars. He figures out how to make water and grow potatoes in poop and martian soil. “The Martian” is never very boring and the development of the situation is interesting. The movie is amazing and I would definitely watch it again. You will be on the edge of your seat the whole time.
